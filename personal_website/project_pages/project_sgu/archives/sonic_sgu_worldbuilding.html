Chaos Dimension

Artifacts

Description

The Chaos Emeralds

The true origins of the Chaos Emeralds is a mystery - in recorded history their earliest origins date back to the Starfall Ancients who brought them to Earth during their encounter with The End. The Chaos Emeralds are gems of unquantifiable power (theorized infinite), and are extremely rare with there only being 7 in existence.

Various civilization conspiracies, tragedies, and downfalls have been attributed to the Chaos Emeralds' involvement, with the connection being those who abuse their power for their own selfish gain - for this reason it’s thought that the Chaos Emeralds can sense the presence of evil in the heart of their user.

In the hands of those with a good-heart the Chaos Emeralds can bring about miracles such as bringing back the dead, and restoring planets that have been torn apart. These emeralds may also be used to achieve an ascended form of the user known as a “Super Form” if the user is of a troubled heart however they have been known to lose their mind or even die if they attempt to achieve it.

The Master Emerald

The Master Emerald is the controller of the Chaos Emeralds as stated in an ancient mantra having the ability to manipulate their energy - spiking them and stopping their energy when needed. This Emerald would eventually find its way into the hands of the Echidna Tribe - of which Pachaccamac would attempt to seize for his own selfish purposes leading his clan’s populus into terminal decline.

Before the Tribe was forgotten to history - they’d put in place a system where someone would be elected the “Master Guardian” this role could be given to someone else but also be passed down to that guardian’s offspring - many generations later Knuckles the Echidna would carry the title of “Master Guardian”.

The Master Emerald has also been known to be powerful enough to manipulate the planet’s tectonic plates which is what caused Newtrogic High to rise out of the ocean it’s energy then entering into the Island’s rings where they crystallize to form the Chaos Rings - rings having powers akin to the Master Emerald to a lesser extent and having only been seen once in the Newtrogic High Incident.

The Super Emeralds

The Super Emeralds are the Chaos Emeralds spiked by Master Emerald Energy causing them to swell in size and power. If the user transforms with these gems they ascend past a Super Form into a Hyper Form, a form far more powerful than in a super form given the ability to breathe underwater and invoke an explosion that destroys anything nearby with no further assistance.

The Super Emeralds however, cannot be summoned on a whim as the Master Emerald must will it. The Master Emerald usually only does so when the Earth is in such a great crisis that the Chaos Emeralds alone cannot neutralize the threat. Attempting to summon the Super Emeralds when the Master Emerald does not will their summoning will cause the Emerald Idols of Angel Island to crack and eventually shatter.

Due to this there’s only been 2 times in recorded history the Super Emeralds have been summoned - during the Angel Island and Starfall Island Incidents.

The Time Stones

The Time Stones are mysterious gems native to Little Planet that can freely manipulate time - unlike the Chaos Emeralds that can only do so in a limited fashion allowing these gems to be capable of great miracles such as turning cast deserts into lush jungles and polluted lakes to crystalline ones. So potent is their power that it’s said those who collect all seven can attain a new form.

In this “Miracle Form” the user is able to both time travel and re-write historical events with just a thought, as such these gems can peer into the user's mind - if it finds the mind impure or cluttered the Time Stone can deny access to this form.

The Phantom Ruby

The Phantom Ruby is the crystalline form of a Chaos Emerald’s Energy after going through the Starfall Ancient’s Cyberspace - as such it’s the result of Cyberspace trying to “interpret” a Chaos Emerald and erroring due to the Infinite Power of the Chaos Emeralds resulting in an incomplete and glitched recreation.

The End would eventually discover this gem and use a small portion of its power to send it through time - resulting in it ending up in the past sometime after the Angel Island Incident, causing disruptions with the Master Emerald.

The Warp Topaz

The Warp Topaz is a gem of unknown origin that has existed since before the World Championship Incident, where it was mistaken as an 8th Chaos Emerald. Sometime after this incident Fang would hear of this and seek it out under belief it was an 8th Chaos Emerald finding it just before the Northstar Island incident.

The Warp Topaz is capable of bending space itself - allowing the user to open portals and windows to faraway areas, however it requires a source of power to do so being an excellent conductor, as a small jolt is enough to allow these feats however it cannot be charged by things more powerful such as the Chaos Emeralds as that will cause it to go haywire.

When the Warp Topaz is out of energy - it will teleport itself to a random cave somewhere in the world to recharge.

Sol Dimension

Artifacts

Description

The Sol Emeralds

The Sol Emeralds are gems of unquantifiable generative power that reside in the Sol Dimension, having powers similar to the Chaos Emeralds - except instead of sensing the evil in one’s heart it senses their “inner flame” and amplifies their power based on that. Their power can thus be used by different entities, when used by an individual they are given a version of the Super Form like with the Chaos Emeralds.

The Sol Emeralds, much like the Chaos Emeralds may also be used to power machines and WMDs, and have a connection to the Sol Dimension on an almost existential level as when not in that dimension the Sol Dimension will gravitate to where the Sol Emeralds are located. The Sol Emeralds also have an odd relationship with the Chaos Emeralds - able to attract and repel them.

When in close proximity the two sets of Emeralds will “call out” to each other - if brought together the Two Sets ofEmeralds will generate cataclysmic power unless the user and/or users knows how to balance their energies, or the Sol Emeralds will themselves to remain stable. The Sol Emeralds may also restore power to a Chaos Emerald that has lost its luster.

The Jeweled Scepter

The Jeweled Scepter is what holds power over the geological activity of the Sol Dimension’s Earth, allowing it to increase volcanic activity, induce earthquakes, and sink active volcanoes. These are all just side effects of it’s immense power however as it’s main ability is unlocking the Power of the Stars.

This power is said to exceed the Chaos Emeralds, and Sol Emeralds - so much so that just a very small fraction of its energy could wipe out the Sol Empire in its entirety. This force having a connection with the scepter is what allows it to tap into its power. This can only be done via ruins underneath Southern Island (Sol Dimension’s counterpart to South Island).

Through this scepters power one may freely manipulate the fabric of space allowing them to manipulate the multiverse or even see/travel to distant realities.

The World Rings

The World Rings are 7 Glass Rings that exist in the world of the Arabian Nights - these rings are intangible to the citizens, passing right through whoever attempts to touch them as such they may only be touched via magic or by beings of another dimension. All 7 World Rings are sealed with an emotion - Hope,Sadness,Rage,Hatred,Joy,Pleasure, and Desire. These emotions being so powerful just touching one jolts the user with a whirlpool of its variants.

The World Rings have been known to drive lower-class djinns insane, these rings are said to be the heart of the stories of the Arabian Nights and as such they are the thing that hold it’s storybook dimension together and open the portal between the Arabian Nights and other dimensions.

South Island

Green Hills

The Green Hills are a series of vast plains, usually tropical in nature spanning across South Island this location is known for it's plantlife and checkerboard soil as far as the eye can see. The plantlife ranges from Palmtrees and Sunflowers to Cherry Blossoms and Merrigolds. The Water flows through the area being so clear people say you can see straight to the bottom - this water being filtered for consumption by the citizens of South Island.

This area is so big it's divided into smaller quadrants (e.g. Green Hill, Emerald Hill, etc.), the residential areas is Emerald Village, a village surrounding a nearby lake with a series of smaller sub-islands in the middle known as the Satura Islands consisting of Resort Island, Radical City, Regal Ruin, and Reactive Factory. These plains would serve as the location to start off Sonic's many adventures.

Great Forest

The Great Forest is a location similar in size to the Green Plains - being a series of sprawling rainforests, home to various flora, fungi, wildlife and rivers/lakes. One of these lakes known as the Lake of Rings dispensing a Harmarusian Gold Ring formed via water erosion known as a Power Ring - a ring that can boost the power of it's user and even power machines.

The Great Forest is home to various different smaller forests but also has a residential area known as Knothole Village ruled under the jurisdiction of the Kingdom of Acorn serving as the refuge for the Freedom Fighters after the Eggman Invasion - since then it serves as a hideout for the Freedom Fihgters in their fight against the forces of Dr. Eggman.

Neo Gigalopolis

The Neo Gigalopolis is a sprawling yet eco-friendly city located in a canyon on South Island, where some of it's citizens live. Many years ago a space station once crash-landed here, having been shut down - the ruins still remain with the Kingdom of Acorn seeking to use it as their personal spacecraft. The area is left in a state of perpetual night, going for months without seeing sunlight - even if it does it's usually brief returning back to nighttime shortly after.

The Neo Gigalopolis encompases Starlight City, Sparkling Spring (Spring Yard + Sparkling), and Gigalopolis .

What Universe does this Timeline focus on?

This timeline focuses on the SEGASonic the Hedgehog Universe (AKA the Core Continuity) as this timeline seeks to chronologically sort the events that occur in the continutity of the Sonic the Hedgehog Video Games from Sonic 1 all the way up to the latest game release however there are a few things to note about this timeline:

  • Remasters Usurp the Original, this is because recent remasters such as Sonic Origins and the upcoming Sonic X Shadow Generations have canonical information that add to the base games.
  • Game Manuals, Developer and Associate Statements will be used as a source of information.
    • This is because Ian Flynn, the current writer for the Games has made statements on the canon of the video games in his podcast - The BumbleKast. This is why the game Sonic Spinball is canon and occurs after Sonic 3 & Knuckles.
  • The Sonic Channel Website depicts this universe as it has comics, artwork, and stories depicting various characters and locations and items in this universe hence it can and will be used for canon information.
    • This is backed by the Sonic the Hedgehog Games as Sonic Channel's "Sonic Comic" features the character Sticks the Badger, who's mentioned by Amy Rose in Sonic Frontiers. This version of Sticks exists alongside characters from this universe such as Silver and Blaze - characters who don't exist in Sonic Boom meaning this Sticks is seperate from the one seen in Sonic Boom.
  • Sonic Prime by WildBrain is confirmed to be canon to the games, with Ian Flynn stating it happens after the events of Sonic Advance 3.
    • Note: Fans debate on this as the series has many contradictions to the games - this however may be a result of mistranslation, consultation of the Japanese script is needed.
  • Context and Story follows the Japanese Versions of the games as usually those are the most accurate versions of the story. English Versions may feature unintentional plot details as a result of mistranslation.
  • SEGA has confirmed that IDW Publishings Sonic the Hedgehog Comic series takes place in the same universe as the Video Games, this is backed by the games as in Sonic Frontiers, Sonic has a line of dialouge mentioning Tangle the Lemur from this comic series. As such some issues will be featured in this Timeline.
  • SEGA YouTube Content can be used to derive information about the Sonic the Hedgehog Video Games Universe as TailsTube #3 Tails tells Sonic he found some strange islands - these being the Starfall Islands from Sonic Frontiers. The two then head off to check them out presumably the events of Sonic Frontiers Prologue: Convergence occurs shortly after, a comic leading directly into the events of the game.
    • This is not limited to SEGA's US Channel either as in a video on SEGA's Japanese YouTube channel titled "Super Sonic Q&A" Sonic recalls an event where Dr. Eggman once stole his shoes replacing them with slow-down boots this being the plot of the SEGA Game Gear Title Sonic Labyrinth
      • The Manuals for Tails Skypatrol and Sonic Labyrinth imply that both games happen concurrently.
    • This also extends to Trailers - as in the Sonic Mania Plus Trailer it can be seen that Knuckles is familiar enough with Mighty the Armadillo to act friendly with him suggesting that Knuckles Chaotix is canon and happens sometime prior.
    • The YouTube Sonic Animated Shorts and some related animations are also canon those being the below, as such they will be featured on this timeline.
      • Sonic Mania Adventures
      • Sonic Superstars: Trio of Trouble!
      • Sonic Night of the Werehog
      • Team Sonic Racing: Overdrive
      • Sonic Frontiers Prologue: Divergence
      • Sonic X Shadow Generations: Dark Beginnings
  • As evidenced by the Japanese Intro message for the Collection Room in Sonic Generations and backed by Sonic Frontiers the Sonic Riders series is canon to the games, this tells us that a game being a spinoff does not disqualify a game from being canon.
  • The SEGASonic The Hedgehog Universe appears to have events adjacent to the Metarex Saga in Sonic X this however does not make Sonic X canon to the games.
    • Metarex Saga: This is because as established earlier Sonic Channel and SEGA Youtube content are canon to the games. In a Sonic Channel Artwork and TailsTube #5 (Remember When?) - a white-potted plant sprout can be seen looking exactly like Cosmo's Heart from Sonic X.
    • Metarex Saga (CONT): Furthermore, in TailsTube #5 (Remember When?) Tails, Sonic and Amy reminice on past adventures many of them being games such as Sonic Superstars and in the Sonic Channel Art the UFO RC from Sonic Lost World can be seen alongside the plant. This suggest adjacent events to the Metarex Saga happened but have yet to be seen, until that story is told it will be left off the timeline.

    • Sonic Generations: Blue Adventure (3DS): This game is unreconcilable with it's console version. Unless we get an offical explanation in Sonic X Shadow Generations or SEGA/Sonic Team explains how one relates to the other. The Collection Room however can be merged with the console version.
